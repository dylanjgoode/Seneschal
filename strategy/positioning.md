# Positioning & Messaging

## One-Paragraph Description (v1 — crypto-native framing)

If you source inventory from international suppliers, you've probably had a card get declined on a payment you've made a dozen times before — not because anything was wrong, but because a fraud model somewhere decided a recurring order to Shenzhen looked suspicious. Tempo Purchasing Agent is an AI that handles your supplier payments over stablecoin rails, where that kind of friction doesn't exist. You tell it what you need to buy; it checks your spending policy, finds the right supplier, and settles the payment in minutes — no card networks, no correspondent banks, no holds. We're running a small pilot with operators who source internationally and are tired of babysitting payments. If that sounds familiar, happy to share a 3-minute demo.

## One-Paragraph Description (v2 — operator-native framing)

Somewhere between you placing an order and your supplier in Shenzhen actually getting paid, a fraud algorithm decides it looks suspicious — and your card gets blocked. It doesn't matter that you've paid them 30 times before. You're now on the phone with your bank, re-sending the payment, and hoping your supplier doesn't give your production slot to someone else. Tempo Purchasing Agent automates your international supplier payments over a direct settlement. No card networks in the middle, no correspondent bank delays, settlement in minutes instead of days. You stay in control of spending limits and approved suppliers; the agent handles the execution and logs everything. We're running a small pilot with operators who source internationally. If the payment friction is real for you, happy to share a quick demo.

## Why now?

Three things converged in the last 18 months that didn't exist before. 

1) First, stablecoin infrastructure matured to the point where you can actually build on it — Stripe acquired Bridge, launched the Machine Payments Protocol on Tempo, and Shopify added native USDC support. This only happened in the last few weeks.

2) Second, AI agents became capable enough to handle the purchasing workflow end-to-end,not just the payment, but supplier selection, order logging, and exception handling. 3 months ago you couldn't build this reliably. 

3) Third, the fraud problem got worse, not better. As more operators automate their operations with AI tools, card networks are seeing more programmatic transaction patterns and tightening their models accordingly. The operators we're targeting are getting squeezed from both sides — more automation, more blocks. We're building at the exact moment the infrastructure became viable and the pain became acute.

## Notes on the two versions

**v1** is tighter and more confident. Better for readers who already have familiarity with crypto/fintech and won't flinch at "stablecoin rails." The mechanism is named upfront.

**v2** buries the mechanism. "Direct digital dollar rail" gives just enough specificity to sound credible without triggering crypto skepticism. It spends more time in the pain — the phone call to the bank, the fear of losing a production slot — which works better with operators who need to feel understood before they trust you.

**Recommendation:** Use v2 for cold outreach and initial emails. Use v1 in conversations with people who've already agreed to a call, or in contexts where the Tempo/crypto angle is a feature not a bug (investor intros, fintech communities).

**CTA note:** Both versions now end with "happy to share a quick demo" instead of asking for 45 minutes. Lower the bar on first contact. You can always expand the conversation once they're interested.

## Design Partner Targets

### Qualifying signals (look for these)

- $2M–$20M revenue, product-based business (e-commerce, CPG, DTC, hardware)
- Sources from 3+ international suppliers regularly
- Has personally felt payment friction — not just heard about it, but has a specific story
- Founder or head of ops — the person who actually places the orders
- Someone in your existing network, or one warm intro away
- Comfortable using unfinished tools and giving honest feedback

### Disqualifying signals (move on quickly)

- Finance-led buying process — they'll want compliance and audit before engaging
- Domestic-only sourcing — the pain doesn't exist for them
- "We use our bank's wire transfer system and it works fine" — no urgency
- Very large companies ($50M+) — procurement process will be too slow for a v0 pilot
- Anyone who asks for a contract or SLA before seeing the product — wrong stage
